Stellar Spectral Synthesis Model

📅 2082–2085 🎯 Generative Modeling 📊 Generated: 100K+ synthetic observations
Generative Adversarial Networks Conditional GANs Generative Models Spectroscopy Data Synthesis

Overview

Stellar spectroscopy is fundamental to astronomy, but obtaining sufficient observational data remains challenging due to telescope time constraints and instrument limitations. This project developed a conditional GAN to generate synthetic but physically plausible stellar spectra, dramatically accelerating the training of downstream astronomy models.

The model has generated over 100,000 high-quality synthetic stellar observations, reducing required observational telescope time by 85% and enabling rapid prototyping of new exoplanet detection algorithms.

Technical Architecture

  • Generator: Conditional deep convolutional network conditioned on stellar parameters (temperature, gravity, metallicity)
  • Discriminator: ResNet-based architecture for spectral authenticity discrimination
  • Conditioning: 8D stellar parameter vector (Teff, logg, [Fe/H], luminosity, mass, age, rotation, extinction)
  • Output: 2048-dimensional spectral flux vectors covering visible to near-infrared wavelengths
  • Training Data: 50K real stellar spectra from GAIA and Kepler archives

Key Results

  • ✓ 100K+ Synthetic Observations Generated with physically realistic properties
  • ✓ 85% Reduction in Observational Requirements for training downstream models
  • ✓ Physics Validation: Generated spectra satisfy fundamental stellar physics constraints
  • ✓ Domain Expert Approval: 94% of synthetic spectra classified as "realistic" by astrophysicists
  • ✓ Downstream Task Improvement: Models trained on synthetic data + limited real data outperform baseline

Applications

The synthetic data has been used to train multiple downstream models in exoplanet detection pipelines, enabling rapid iteration and experimentation without consuming precious telescope time. New researchers joining the team can now quickly prototype algorithms on abundant synthetic data before moving to scarce real observations.
